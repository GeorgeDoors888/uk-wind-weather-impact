# UK Energy Maps - Interconnector Guide

## Overview

The UK has 12 operational and planned power interconnectors linking to mainland Europe, Ireland, and Scandinavia. These submarine cables allow electricity to flow in both directions, enhancing energy security and enabling renewable energy trading.

## Strategic Importance

1. **Energy Security:** Diversified electricity sources
2. **Renewable Integration:** Import wind from Denmark, hydro from Norway
3. **Price Optimization:** Trade based on supply/demand differentials
4. **Grid Stability:** Backup during UK outages or high demand
5. **Carbon Reduction:** Access to low-carbon European electricity

## Technical Details

- **Technology:** High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) transmission
- **Voltage Levels:** Typically 320-525 kV
- **Depth:** Up to 60 meters seabed depth
- **Bidirectional:** Power flows both ways depending on market conditions
- **Efficiency:** ~95% transmission efficiency for HVDC
